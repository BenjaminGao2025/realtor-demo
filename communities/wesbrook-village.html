Wesbrook Village: UBC's Premier Residential Community

A master-planned village on UBC's south campus — where modern living, top-rated schools, and natural beauty come together.

Wesbrook Village Overview

Wesbrook Village is UBC's flagship master-planned residential community, located on the university's south campus between Pacific Spirit Regional Park and the academic core. What began in the early 2000s as an ambitious vision to create a complete, walkable neighborhood on university land has matured into one of Metro Vancouver's most desirable places to live — a vibrant village of roughly 15,000 residents that combines the convenience of urban living with the tranquility of a campus setting bordered by old-growth forest and ocean views.

The village's design follows a new-urbanist philosophy: medium-density residential buildings arranged around a central commercial high street, with parks, greenways, and pedestrian pathways woven throughout. Unlike the broader UBC campus area, which encompasses older neighborhoods like Hampton Place and Chancellor Place, Wesbrook Village was planned from the ground up as a cohesive community with its own commercial centre, school, childcare facilities, and recreational spaces. The result is a neighborhood where nearly everything you need for daily life is within a five-minute walk from your front door.

Wesbrook Village continues to evolve as UBC releases new parcels for development. The community is currently home to over a dozen completed residential buildings — including notable developments such as Sitka, Hub, CentreBlock, Tapestry, Ponderosa Commons, and Alloy — with additional phases planned over the coming decade. Each new building adds architectural variety and housing options to the neighborhood, from compact one-bedroom units ideal for young professionals and students to spacious townhouses designed for growing families.

Property Types & Prices

Wesbrook Village offers a focused range of housing primarily consisting of concrete condominiums and townhouses. Because all residential land here is owned by UBC and sold on 99-year prepaid leases, prices tend to be somewhat lower than comparable freehold properties in neighboring Point Grey — making it an attractive entry point for buyers seeking a UBC-area address. Here's a snapshot of current pricing:

Newer buildings in Wesbrook Village, such as Alloy and Tapestry, feature contemporary designs with high-end finishes, energy-efficient systems, and generous amenity packages including rooftop terraces, fitness centres, and co-working lounges. Older buildings like Sitka and Hub, while slightly more modest in amenities, offer competitive pricing and established strata management. Two-bedroom units in recently completed buildings typically command a 10–15% premium over resale units in the village's earlier phases. Pre-sale opportunities in upcoming developments provide buyers the chance to lock in pricing before construction completion.

Schools & Education

For families, Wesbrook Village's school catchment is one of the most compelling reasons to buy here. Norma Rose Point Elementary, opened in 2017 and located at the heart of the village, is a state-of-the-art facility offering English and French Immersion programs from Kindergarten through Grade 7. The school features modern learning spaces, Indigenous design elements reflecting UBC's commitment to reconciliation, and sustainable building practices that earned it LEED Gold certification. Its location within Wesbrook Village means most students can walk to school in under five minutes — a rarity in Vancouver's real estate landscape.

For secondary education, University Hill Secondary (grades 8–12) serves the broader UBC community and is known for its strong academics, Advanced Placement courses, and exceptional university acceptance rates. Graduates regularly earn admission to UBC, the University of Toronto, McGill, and leading international universities. The school benefits from its proximity to UBC's research facilities, offering unique enrichment opportunities that few other high schools can match.

Being within the UBC school catchment adds a measurable premium to property values — homes in-catchment for Norma Rose Point Elementary and University Hill Secondary typically sell for 10–15% more than comparable properties just outside the boundary. UBC also operates extensive childcare facilities across campus, including multiple centres within walking distance of Wesbrook Village serving infants through school-age children, which is a significant advantage for working families.

Lifestyle & Amenities

Wesbrook Village functions as a self-contained neighborhood thanks to its well-stocked commercial centre. The village high street is anchored by a full-size Save-On-Foods supermarket and complemented by a diverse mix of restaurants, cafés, a pharmacy, dental and medical clinics, banking services, a liquor store, and a variety of specialty shops. From morning coffee at a local café to an evening dinner with friends, residents can meet most daily needs without leaving the neighborhood. A weekly farmers market during the growing season brings fresh local produce, artisanal goods, and a community-gathering atmosphere to the village plaza.

Recreation is woven into the fabric of Wesbrook Village. Pacific Spirit Regional Park, with over 73 kilometres of trails through temperate rainforest, is accessible directly from the village's western edge — ideal for running, cycling, dog-walking, and nature exploration. The UBC Aquatic Centre offers a 50-metre Olympic pool, while the Doug Mitchell Thunderbird Sports Centre provides ice rinks and fitness facilities. Residents also enjoy easy access to the Chan Centre for the Performing Arts, the Museum of Anthropology, the UBC Botanical Garden, and Wreck Beach — all within a short walk or bike ride from home.

Transit connectivity is strong and improving. The 99 B-Line and R4 RapidBus routes connect Wesbrook Village to the Broadway Subway and broader SkyTrain network. Bus stops within the village provide direct service to downtown Vancouver in approximately 35–45 minutes. Cycling infrastructure includes protected bike lanes along University Boulevard, and car-sharing services (Modo, Evo) have vehicles stationed on campus for those who prefer not to own a car.

Investment Value

Wesbrook Village offers a compelling investment case built on several structural advantages. The 99-year prepaid land lease model, while sometimes misunderstood, is a key factor: lease terms are long enough to support mortgage financing from all major Canadian banks, and the lower entry price compared to freehold properties in Point Grey or Kitsilano means stronger rental yields. With UBC's population of over 65,000 students and 15,000 faculty and staff creating persistent rental demand, vacancy rates in Wesbrook Village hover near zero at the start of each academic year.

The most significant catalyst on the horizon is the planned SkyTrain Millennium Line extension to UBC, with a target completion in the early 2030s. A proposed station near Wesbrook Village would fundamentally transform connectivity, reducing transit time to downtown Vancouver to approximately 20 minutes. Historically, rapid transit extensions in Metro Vancouver have boosted nearby property values by 10–20% in the first years of operation. For Wesbrook Village — already well-positioned with strong schools, amenities, and rental demand — the SkyTrain extension represents a potential inflection point for capital appreciation.

Historical price trends in Wesbrook Village have been consistently positive, with values appreciating 5–7% annually over the past decade. The limited supply of buildable land within the UBC endowment, combined with sustained demand from families, investors, and university-affiliated buyers, creates a structural supply-demand imbalance that supports long-term price growth. For buyers seeking a combination of livability, rental income potential, and future upside, Wesbrook Village remains one of the strongest micro-markets within the broader UBC area.
